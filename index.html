Shopify POS

Self-Serve Migration Guide

Everything you need to migrate from your current POS system to Shopify POS. Work through it at your own pace — most merchants complete setup in 1-2 weeks.

Shopify Plus POS Pro Active Unified Commerce

Before You Start

Grab these exports from your current POS system before building anything in Shopify. Having this data ready makes everything faster.

Phase 1

Export Your Data ~1 day

Pull these from your current POS. Save everything as CSV files where possible — that's what Shopify uses for imports.

  • Product catalog — name, SKU, price, barcode, quantity per location
  • Customer list — name, email, phone, address
  • Active gift card balances and codes (if applicable)
  • Current tax settings per location
  • Custom discounts or loyalty programs — document how they work today
  • Hardware inventory — note your current receipt printers, scanners, cash drawers (some may be compatible with Shopify)

Set Up Your Locations

Phase 2

Configure Retail Locations ~15 min

Your Shopify admin already has POS Pro active. Now make sure your retail location(s) are configured correctly.

  1. Go to Settings → Locations
  2. Confirm your retail location is listed with the correct name and address. If not, click "Add location" and enter your store details.
  3. For each retail location, enable "Fulfill online orders from this location" if you want ship-from-store capability.
  4. Verify the correct inventory is assigned to each location.
Multiple locations? Repeat for each store. Every location gets its own inventory, staff assignments, and POS settings.

Activate Shopify Payments

Phase 3

Enable Card-Present Payments ~30 min

Required for accepting card payments at POS. Without this, you can only take cash.

  1. Go to Settings → Payments
  2. Click "Activate Shopify Payments" (if not already active)
  3. Complete business verification — business info, bank account for deposits, tax ID
  4. Once approved, your POS card reader will process payments through Shopify Payments automatically
Good to know: Card-present transactions are decoupled from Shopify's platform — your register keeps processing cards even during a backend hiccup. Deposits typically hit your bank account in 2 business days.

Import Products & Inventory

Phase 4

Get Your Catalog Into Shopify 1-3 days

If your products are already in Shopify from your online store, you just need to make sure inventory quantities are correct at your retail location(s).

  1. Go to Products → Inventory
  2. Filter by your retail location
  3. Update quantities to match what's physically on the shelf
  4. For bulk updates: Products → Export → update the CSV → Import

If your products are NOT in Shopify yet:

  1. Go to Products → Import
  2. Upload your CSV — use Shopify's product CSV template from the Help Center
  3. Map your columns and import
  4. After import, go to Inventory and set quantities per location
Barcodes matter. If you have barcodes in your current system, make sure they're included in your CSV. Shopify POS uses them for scanning at checkout — saves your staff from searching by name.

Import Customers

Phase 5

Bring Over Your Customer Data ~30 min

  1. Go to Customers → Import
  2. Upload your CSV — include name, email, phone, address, and any tags
  3. Shopify will merge duplicates by email address automatically

This gives your staff access to customer profiles at the register — purchase history, contact info, and order lookup all in one place.

Migrate Gift Cards

Phase 6

Transfer Outstanding Balances varies

Skip this section if you don't have gift cards to migrate.

  1. Go to Products → Gift cards → Add gift card
  2. Enter the same code from your old gift cards — Shopify accepts custom codes
  3. Set the balance to match what's outstanding
  4. Leave the "Customer" field blank — filling it triggers a notification email you don't want for legacy cards
  5. Once all cards are in Shopify, disable the codes in your old system immediately
Important: Do not run both systems in parallel for gift cards — it will cause balance mismatches. Disable old codes at cutover. For bulk imports (50+ cards), use the Shopify Admin API or an app like Rise.AI.

Set Up Your Staff

Phase 7

Add Team Members & Set Permissions ~1 hour

  1. Go to Settings → Users and permissions (or Point of Sale → Staff)
  2. Click "Add staff" — for POS-only staff, add them as POS staff (these don't count against your admin staff seats)
  3. Go to Point of Sale → Roles and create roles for your team:
    RoleTypical Permissions
    CashierProcess sales, scan items, accept payments
    Shift Lead+ Apply discounts, process returns
    Manager+ Void transactions, view reports, edit orders
  4. Assign each staff member to their location(s)
  5. Staff will create their own PIN on first login to the POS app
Tip: Restrict discount and refund permissions to managers or shift leads if you want tighter controls on the floor.

Set Up Your Hardware

Phase 8

Get Your Register Ready 1-2 days

POS App:

  1. Download "Shopify POS" from the App Store (iPad/iPhone) or Google Play (Android)
  2. Sign in with your Shopify credentials
  3. Select your location when prompted

Card Reader:

  1. Order your card reader from hardware.shopify.com (use any discount code provided)
  2. Once it arrives, open the POS app → Settings → Card Reader → Connect
  3. Follow the Bluetooth pairing instructions

Test Before You Go Live

Phase 9

Run Through Every Workflow ~2 hours

Before your first real customer transaction, run through each of these:

  • Cash sale — process a sale, verify receipt prints correctly
  • Card sale — verify card reader connects and payment goes through
  • Return / refund — process a return, verify refund hits the correct payment method
  • Discount — apply a discount, verify it calculates correctly
  • Barcode scan — scan an item, verify it pulls up the right product
  • Customer lookup — search for a customer, verify their profile loads
  • Gift card — redeem a gift card, verify balance deducts correctly
  • Register shift — open and close a shift, verify cash tracking works
  • Staff permissions — test PIN login and role restrictions for each role
First real card transaction: Use a small dollar amount to confirm deposits hit your bank account. Deposits typically arrive in 2 business days.

Go-Live Day

Phase 10

Make the Switch ~half day

Pick a slower day of the week for cutover so your team has room to get comfortable without peak-hour pressure.

  1. Do a final inventory count at your retail location
  2. Update Shopify inventory to match the physical count
  3. Disable card processing on your old POS so there's no confusion about which register is live
  4. Process your first real transactions on Shopify POS
  5. Keep your old POS system accessible but not active for 2-4 weeks in case you need to reference historical data

After Launch

Phase 11

First Week Checks

  • Check your first Shopify invoice — Settings → Billing — confirm charges look right
  • Verify your first deposit hits your bank account
  • Review the Sales by Staff report to make sure transactions are attributing correctly
  • Check inventory levels after a few days of selling — confirm sync is working
